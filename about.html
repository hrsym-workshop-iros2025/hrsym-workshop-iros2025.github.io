Motion Dataset

Here is some information explaining the specs and formats of the Motion Dataset. Check out the benchmarks based on this dataset: Motion Prediction, Interaction Prediction.

Overview

The motion dataset is provided as sharded pickle format files containing numpy array data. The data are split into training, test, and validation sets with a split of 70% training, 15% testing and 15% validation data.


The dataset is composed of 103,354 612,000 each containing 60 seconds of object tracks at 10Hz and map data for the area covered by the segment. These segments are further broken into 9 second windows (1 second of history and 8 seconds of future data) with varying overlap. The data is provided in two forms. One form involves scene data containing approximately 60 seconds of raw data, which includes annotated interaction information between two vehicles. The other form consists of scene data split into 9.1-second segments, where the interested agents within the scenes are labeled and can be used for training the latest algorithms.

Like Waymo Open Motion Dataset, to enable the motion prediction challenge or interaction prediction challenge, the ground truth future data for the test set is hidden from challenge participants. As such, the test sets contain only 1 second of history data. The training and validation sets contain the ground truth future data for use in model development. In addition, the test and validation sets provide a list of up to 8 object tracks in the scene to be predicted. These are selected to include interesting behavior and a balance of object types.


Data Sampling

Each 9 second sequence in either the training or validation set contains 1 second of history data, 1 sample for the current time, and 8 seconds of future data at 10 Hz sampling. This corresponds to 10 history samples, 1 current time sample, and 80 future samples for a total of 91 samples. The test set hides the ground truth future data for a total of 11 samples (10 history and 1 current time sample).


Coordinate frames

All coordinates in the dataset are in a global frame with X as East, Y as North and Z as up. The origin of the coordinate system changes in each scene. The origin is an arbitrary point and may be far from the objects in the scene. All units are in meters.
