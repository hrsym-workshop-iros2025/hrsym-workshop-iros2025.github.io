Abstract of Yiwei Wang's Talk

Talk title: Mixed Reality for Human-Centric Smart Manufacturing.

Abstract: As an emerging manufacturing paradigm, Industry 5.0 emphasizes human-centric intelligent manufacturing. Mixed Reality (MR) technology brings unprecedented opportunities for assembly and maintenance in such manufacturing paradigm. MR innovates product assembly by overlaying intuitive visual instructions onto the physical workspace, improving operational efficiency. This presentation reports the befits that MR brings to large-scale products assembly in aerospace industry, which suffers problems such as labor intensive, large spatial span, wide range of factors involved, poor coordination performance, low degree of automation. A series technologies including MR-based robot path preview, MR visualization guidance, multi-person collaborative, holographic sharing, instrument interoperability, multimodal interaction, MR representation authoring etc. has been developed. This "assembly-measurement-adjustment" closed-loop process enables correct assembly errors in time and reduce rework. For the problem of information shortage or overload during the MR-guided assembly, we proposed AR-AIP, an MR-guided adaptive information push method for product assembly based on context-awareness, which aligns information load with human cognitive abilities. The above key technologies are integrated into the developed MR-assisted assembly system and have been used in the assembly of aerospace products such as large-scale space deployable mechanism, flexible fixture of aircraft and missile barrel etc. The results show that the proposed method and the developed MR-assisted assembly system are able to alleviate cognitive load, reduce rework, and improve the assembly efficiency and quality of aerospace products.
