Abstract of Peng (Edward) Wang's Talk

Talk title: Harnessing Plant Data for Unleashing the Power of AI on the Shop Floor of Robotic Welding.

Abstract: Machine learning (ML) models trained on limited labeled lab data often struggle to generalize across diverse robotic welding systems, materials, and dynamic process conditions. In contrast, vast amounts of plant data from robotic welding operations remain largely unlabeled, making supervised learning impractical. This work introduces a multi-scale contrastive learning (MSCL) framework to extract meaningful representations from unlabeled plant data, enabling the development of highly adaptable AI models for intelligent robotic welding. Our MSCL approach leverages meta-information—such as material stack-up, electrode conditions, and robotic process parameters—to guide representation learning. It employs contrastive learning techniques at multiple scales—instance-level and meta-level—to structure the feature space effectively. At the instance level, contrastive loss ensures that embeddings of similar welding instances (e.g., from the same material stack-up) are pulled closer, while dissimilar instances are pushed apart. At the group level, hierarchical clustering techniques further refine the feature space to align with the underlying robotic welding process dynamics. This enables AI models to learn robust feature representations without explicit labels.

By training on large-scale, real-world robotic welding data, the resulting ML model is both adaptive to varying process conditions and versatile across downstream applications, including defect detection and weld quality prediction. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach enables expulsion detection in novel material stack-ups without fine-tuning or transfer learning. For quality prediction tasks, it outperforms conventional transfer learning approaches, achieving superior accuracy and robustness. Beyond resistance spot welding, this methodology has significant potential for other robotic welding processes, such as laser welding in EV battery manufacturing, where label scarcity and process variability present similar challenges.
